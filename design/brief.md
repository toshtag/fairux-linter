# Project Brief

## What we're building

FairUX Linter is a rule-based, explainable linter that detects UI patterns which may distort informed user decision-making: dark patterns, misleading subscription flows, hidden costs, unfair consent UI, and scarcity pressure. It scans static HTML today via a runtime-agnostic, browser-safe core designed to extend to other surfaces (DOM, AST, Figma) later.

## Who it's for

Developers, designers, and UX reviewers who want to catch decision-distorting UI before release, and teams integrating UX checks into CLI, CI, and editor/browser workflows over time.

## What makes it different

A deterministic, browser-safe, explainable rules engine. Not an AI-first auditor and not a legal judgment tool: findings are UX risk signals for human review, with explicit evidence and stable fingerprints.
